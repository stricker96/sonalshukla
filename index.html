About Me

I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CCBAP) at the State University of New York at Buffalo. My research lies at the intersection of cutting-edge technologies in deep learning, computer vision, federated learning, and privacy-preserving artificial intelligence, with a particular focus on applications in medical imaging and digital pathology.

Over the course of my doctoral studies, I have explored algorithm design for distributed systems, edge computing, and blockchain-based frameworks to enhance the security, efficiency, and scalability of AI-driven solutions. My work is driven by a central goal: to develop robust, interpretable, and privacy-aware machine learning systems that can be deployed in real-world healthcare settings.

Currently, I am developing a comprehensive digital imaging and analysis tool aimed at improving cancer detection and diagnostic workflows. This involves working with Whole Slide Imaging (WSI) data and leveraging AI to assist in early detection and decision support in oncology. My research addresses critical challenges in medical AI, including data privacy, model generalization, and the interpretability of results in high-stakes environments.
